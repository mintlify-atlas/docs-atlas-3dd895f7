## Create Your First Timeline

Once you've installed the app, follow these steps to create your first timeline:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the App">
    Launch Memento Calendar. On first launch, you'll see an empty home screen with a prompt to create a timeline.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tap the Create Button">
    Tap the **+** button or the "Create Timeline" prompt to open the timeline creation form.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose a Timeline Type">
    Select one of four timeline types:
    - **Year** - Track the entire current year (365 days)
    - **Month** - Track the current month
    - **Week** - Track the current week (auto-updates weekly)
    - **Custom** - Create a timeline for any date range
    
    For your first timeline, we recommend starting with **Week** to see immediate results.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review and Save">
    The timeline preview will show you how many days are in the period. Tap **Save** to create your timeline.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="View Your Grid">
    Your new timeline will appear on the home screen as a grid of squares. Each square represents one day, with filled squares showing days that have passed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the Grid

The grid is the heart of Memento Calendar. Here's how to read it:

- **Filled squares** = Days that have passed
- **Empty squares** = Days remaining
- **Grid layout** = Automatically calculated to fit your screen
- **One square = One day** = Each square always represents exactly one real day

<Tip>
  Tap on any square to see the specific date it represents. This helps you understand exactly where you are in your timeline.
</Tip>

## Managing Multiple Timelines

You can create and track multiple timelines simultaneously:

1. **Tap the timeline dropdown** at the top of the home screen to see all your timelines
2. **Switch active timelines** by tapping any timeline in the dropdown
3. **Edit timelines** by opening the management screen (tap the list icon)
4. **Delete timelines** by swiping left on any timeline in the management screen

<Warning>
  Free users can create up to 3 timelines. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited timelines.
</Warning>

## Week Timeline Auto-Update

Week timelines are special—they automatically update:

- The timeline always shows the **current week** (Monday through Sunday)
- When a new week starts, the dates automatically refresh
- You don't need to recreate week timelines manually

This makes week timelines perfect for maintaining awareness of the present moment.
