## The Stoic Grid

At the heart of Stoic Calendar is the grid visualization system. The grid transforms abstract time into something concrete and visible.

<Info>
**Core principle**: 1 dot = 1 day. This never changes.
</Info>

## How It Works

Every timeline is rendered as a grid of squares (dots), where:

- **Filled dots** represent days that have already passed
- **Empty dots** represent days that are still in the future

As each day passes, one more dot fills in. This creates a visual representation of time's passage that is truthful and non-negotiable.

## Design Principles

The grid follows strict visualization rules based on Stoic Calendar's design philosophy:

### Truthful Visualization

- One dot always equals one real day
- No grouping of days
- No averages or abstractions
- No shortcuts

<Note>
A year timeline always shows 365 or 366 individual dots. A week timeline always shows exactly 7 dots.
</Note>

### Dynamic Layout

The grid automatically adapts to fit its container:

- **Dot size** scales based on total days and screen size
- **Spacing** adjusts proportionally
- **Grid dimensions** are calculated to optimize layout
- All timelines fit within the viewport without scrolling

<Tip>
The grid layout utilities in `utils/grid-layout.ts` handle responsive calculations automatically, so grids look perfect on any screen size.
</Tip>

### Minimal Styling

Grids follow the quiet UI principle:

- No decorative elements
- No rounded containers around the grid
- No gradients or shadows
- Clean, unadorned squares

## What's NOT Allowed

To maintain the integrity of the visualization:

- **No scrollable grids in widgets**: The entire timeline must fit at once
- **No grouped dots**: Every day remains individual
- **No visual noise**: The grid speaks for itself

<Warning>
These restrictions are intentional. They preserve the clarity and honesty of the visualization.
</Warning>

## Grid States

Each dot in the grid has only two states:

1. **Filled**: Day has passed (uses accent color)
2. **Empty**: Day is in the future (uses background color)

There are no intermediate states, no partial fills, no special indicators. This binary simplicity is what makes the grid powerful.

## Color System

The grid uses Apple's semantic color system:

- **Background colors**: `systemBackground`, `secondarySystemBackground`
- **Labels**: `label`, `secondaryLabel`
- **Accent**: Single cool blue/indigo tone for filled dots

<Info>
Colors automatically adapt to light and dark mode, ensuring the grid remains visible in any environment.
</Info>

## Animation

Grids may include subtle fill animation when first rendered:

- Linear or ease-in-out timing only
- No bounce or spring effects
- No attention-grabbing transitions

<Note>
Widgets may be fully static. Animation is optional and always subtle.
</Note>

## The Philosophy Behind the Grid

The grid exists to make time **visible**, not actionable:

- It doesn't tell you what to do
- It doesn't motivate or warn
- It doesn't pressure you to be productive

The grid simply shows reality: days passing, one at a time, with no judgment.
